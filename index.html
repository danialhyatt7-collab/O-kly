The Material

Solid oak, grain to grain.

We build with kiln-dried, solid oak — no veneers, no particleboard. Every frame is joined by hand using traditional mortise-and-tenon joinery, then finished with a hand-rubbed oil that deepens the grain instead of hiding it.

It's why an Oakly piece can be sanded down and refinished for decades instead of thrown out — built to be repaired, not replaced.

Care Guide

Built to last — if you look after it

Solid oak ages with you. A little care keeps it looking new for decades.

Wipe, don't soak

Dust with a dry or slightly damp soft cloth. Avoid standing water and harsh cleaners — they dull the finish over time.

Re-oil yearly

A yearly coat of hard-wax oil keeps the grain fed and the surface protected — the same finish we use in the workshop.

Keep it out of direct sun

Extended direct sunlight can unevenly fade oak — an occasional rotation keeps the colour consistent.
